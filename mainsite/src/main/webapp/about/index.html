Fear of Software lives in Santiago, Chile with roots in New York City. We seek to create more enriching experiences in the medium of computing and on the web. Our current work is to change the way in which people receive new information and content as others create it online.


Alex Boland

Alexander Boland is a hacker/writer cum designer from Brooklyn whose interests lie at the intersection of narratives and computer science and aims to create more meaningful interactions with software through humanistic approaches derived from literary theory, folk psychology and semiotics. He graduated from Oberlin College in 2010 with a degree in English and Computer Science with departmental honors for his thesis on Interactive Storytelling.

Nick LaRacuente

Nick LaRacuente focuses on making sure that Fear of Software stays in touch with the rest of the world. He enjoys being on stage, writing for fun and trying new things. Nick's past inventions include a software-based real-time autocorrelator and a tool for hacking raw Scheme code without the need to enter more than single words of text.

Nick has a BA with high honors in physics from Swarthmore College.
